How AI Agents Are Quietly Rewiring Knowledge Work

By Kashmir Hill · April 10, 2026 · 8 min read

For years, productivity software promised to organize our work. The new generation of AI agents promises something different — to actually do it.

Across industries, teams are quietly handing off repetitive tasks to software that can read, write, decide, and follow up. The results are surprising even the people who built them.

"We thought this would save people ten minutes a day," said one executive. "It's saving them ten hours a week."

The shift has implications for how knowledge workers spend their time — and whether entire categories of software will need to reinvent themselves.
